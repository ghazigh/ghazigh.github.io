Who am I?

About me

I am a Ph.D. student at ÉTS Montréal supervised by professors Georges Kaddoum, specializing in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and 6G vehicular networks (V2X). My research focuses on leveraging AI-driven technologies to address challenges in next-generation network management, particularly in the areas of digital twin and multimodal collaborative perception for 6G V2X network management and radio resource management. My recent work includes the development of AI solutions using Vision Transformers for 6G vehicular networks.

My academic journey has been shaped by hands-on experiences across various industries, including the development of AI-based property valuation models and NLP solutions for healthcare. I have also contributed to research on the robustness of AI classifiers against adversarial attacks during my time as a visiting student at the University of California, Irvine. With a strong background in deep learning, my work bridges practical AI applications with theoretical advancements.

Outside of research, I have a passion for teaching and mentoring, currently serving as a Teaching Assistant at ÉTS. With a commitment to advancing the field of AI and its real-world applications, I aim to continue contributing to both academia and industry.
