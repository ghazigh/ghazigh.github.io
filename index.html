§ 01
Profile

Who am I, really?

A short self-portrait — research threads, practitioner habits, and why teaching keeps me honest.

I recently defended my Ph.D. at ÉTS Montréal, under the supervision of Prof. Georges Kaddoum — with an Excellent mention for both the thesis and the defense, and a nomination by the jury for the institutional Excellence Award. My dissertation, Multimodal Collaborative MARL for 6G V2X Digital Twins, argues that the next generation of vehicular networks will not simply be faster radios — they will be perceptive systems in which cameras, LiDAR and the channel itself are treated as one joint signal that agents learn to read and act on together.

Day-to-day, I work at the seam between research and product. As Principal Agentic AI Developer at CDPQ, I design and ship multi-agent platforms for one of the largest institutional investors in the world: planner-executor architectures, retrieval pipelines, MCP servers, evaluation harnesses — the pieces that let LLM agents plan, use tools and complete multi-step tasks reliably at the scale of a financial institution.

“

My north star is to turn strong ideas into systems that are measurable, trustworthy, and used — in papers, in production, and in people's daily workflow.

Before CDPQ I spent six years bouncing between labs and startups — visiting researcher at UC Irvine on adversarial robustness, Mitacs-funded intern on deep-learning-based property valuation, NLP engineer building a clinical chatbot, graph-partitioning ML for FPGA emulation at Mentor Graphics. Those years taught me to respect the distance between a notebook and a deployment, and to enjoy crossing it.

I also care deeply about how knowledge travels. I teach at ÉTS, I mentor students and junior engineers, and I write & podcast about what I learn. Clarity-first isn't a slogan for me — it's the only way I know to make complex AI useful.

§ 05
Bibliography

Peer-reviewed publications.

For the freshest list see my Google Scholar profile.

  1. [P-04] 2025

    Multimodal Collaborative Perception for Dynamic Channel Prediction in 6G V2X Networks.

    G. Gharsallah & G. Kaddoum
    IEEE Transactions on Machine Learning in Communications and Networking

    Shows that fusing camera, LiDAR and radar across cooperating vehicles — rather than relying on single-sensor views — substantially improves short-horizon wireless channel prediction under real V2X mobility.

  2. [P-03] 2025

    Multimodal Collaborative Perception for 6G V2X Power and Spectrum Management.

    G. Gharsallah & G. Kaddoum
    ICC 2025 — IEEE International Conference on Communications

    Closes the loop between perception and radio resource management: collaborative multimodal features drive a joint power-and-spectrum policy that outperforms channel-only baselines in throughput and interference.

  3. [P-02] 2024

    MVX-ViT: Multimodal Collaborative Perception for 6G V2X Network Management Decisions Using Vision Transformer.

    G. Gharsallah & G. Kaddoum
    IEEE Open Journal of the Computer Society

    Introduces a Vision-Transformer architecture that treats heterogeneous V2X sensors as a unified token stream, learning end-to-end network-management decisions directly from raw multimodal evidence.

  4. [P-01] 2023

    ViT-LoS V2X: Vision Transformers for Environment-Aware LoS Blockage Prediction for 6G Vehicular Networks.

    G. Gharsallah & G. Kaddoum
    IEEE Access

    First work to bring Vision Transformers to line-of-sight / blockage prediction for 6G vehicular links, demonstrating that spatial attention on the urban scene generalises better than CNNs across intersections and weather.

  5. [B-00] 2020

    AI Classifiers' Robustness to Adversarial Attacks — Manipulating Output of Classifiers by Small Input Perturbations.

    G. Gharsallah & Z. Wang
    Bachelor thesis · UC Irvine, supervised by Prof. Zhiying Wang

    Foundational study that shaped how I think about AI evaluation today: how imperceptible input perturbations flip classifier decisions, and what it takes to measure — not just claim — robustness.

Selected works

Projects & open source.

W-04 3D Viz · Research Discovery

IEEE Vis Explorer

An interactive 3D map of 37 000+ IEEE papers across 9 topical clusters. Abstracts are embedded, projected into three dimensions, and clustered by semantic similarity — so you can fly through a decade of research, colour by topic or citation impact, filter by journal, and search for any paper title in real time. Built as a fast, lightweight web app that turns a static bibliography into a navigable landscape.

React · TypeScript · Three.js · R3F · Zustand View on GitHub ↗
W-03 Agentic · LLM

Agentic News System

A multi-agent platform that autonomously reads financial news and turns it into investable signals. A planner decomposes a macro question, tool-using agents gather evidence from news, filings and market data, and a reflective critic agent challenges the draft thesis before anything is surfaced to a human analyst.

Python · LangGraph · Azure OpenAI · MCP
W-02 GenAI · 6G

Generative 6G V2X Traffic

A generative digital twin for the vehicular radio channel: given a scene and a mobility pattern, it synthesises plausible time-varying channel traces that would be expensive — or simply impossible — to collect in the real world. Used as a stress-test harness for perception and RRM policies.

PyTorch · Diffusion · SUMO · Ray Tracing
W-01 Co-simulation · Flagship

MVX — multimodal V2X co-simulation

To my knowledge, the first configurable, scalable co-simulation framework that marries a realistic physics-based world simulator with a differentiable ray-traced wireless simulator. MVX generates multi-agent, multimodal datasets (camera, LiDAR, radar, channel) for AI-driven digital twins in vehicular communications — the backbone of most of my Ph.D. experiments and the substrate on which collaborative perception research can actually be reproduced.

Python · CARLA · Ray Tracing · Gym · Multi-agent Visit project ↗
