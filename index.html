Who am I?

About me

Research profile. I’m a Ph.D. student at ÉTS Montréal, supervised by Georges Kaddoum, working at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and 6G vehicular networks (V2X). My research explores how AI can make next-generation networks more reliable and adaptive, especially through digital twins, multimodal collaborative perception, and smarter radio resource management. A key thread in my recent work is applying modern deep learning (including Vision Transformers) to real V2X constraints.

Professional profile. Beyond the lab, I enjoy building practical AI systems end-to-end, from data pipelines and modeling to evaluation and deployment. Lately, a big focus has been agentic AI: designing assistants and workflows that can plan, use tools, retrieve knowledge, and reliably execute multi-step tasks with clear guardrails and measurable performance. I’ve worked on applied projects such as AI-based property valuation and NLP solutions for healthcare, and I’ve contributed to research on the robustness of AI classifiers against adversarial attacks as a visiting student at the University of California, Irvine. My goal is to turn strong ideas into systems that are usable, measurable, and impactful.

Teaching & other activities. I care a lot about knowledge transfer: mentoring, simplifying complex topics, and helping others grow. I currently serve as a Teaching Assistant at ÉTS, and I bring the same “clarity-first” mindset to collaborations, whether it’s supporting students, contributing to a team, or sharing what I learn through hands-on projects.
